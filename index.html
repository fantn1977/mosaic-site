Why Mosaic Exists

I built this because I needed it.

I spent a year training seriously. Consistent workouts, better sleep, watching the numbers move in the right direction. It worked. I got into the best shape of my life.

Then I needed surgery.

The recovery reset everything. When I was ready to start moving again, I had all the same devices, all the same apps — and none of them could answer the questions that actually mattered.

What was my peak fitness level when I was training regularly?

Where am I right now?

How will I know when I've actually gotten back to where I was?

Every app told me today's numbers. None of them had a memory. None of them knew where I'd come from or how far I had to go.

So I built Mosaic.

The Gap

Your apps track today.
They don't remember your best.

Health tracking has gotten remarkably good at measuring the present. Your heart rate. Your sleep. Your recovery score this morning.

What it hasn't solved is context. Without knowing your personal peak — the best sustained performance your body has actually demonstrated — every number is just a number. You don't know if 72 is good or bad. You don't know if you're improving or declining. You don't know how close you are to where you used to be.

Most apps skip this problem entirely. Mosaic was built around it.

The Answer

Three questions.
Finally answered.

01

What was my peak?

Mosaic establishes your personal baseline — the best your data has actually observed, across all sources, over time. Not a population average. Not an estimate. Your real ceiling, built from your real history.

This is the reference point everything else is measured against.

02

Where am I now?

Your Mosaic Score is a single number — 0 to 100 — showing how close you are to that baseline today. Built from multiple signals across your health data: training load, recovery, sleep, resting heart rate.

And unlike most scores, it shows you the ranked drivers behind it. Not just what the number is — why it is.

03

How do I know when I'm back?

When your Mosaic Score consistently trends toward your baseline, you're getting back. When it holds there, you're there.

No guessing. No comparing yourself to strangers. Just you, measured against the best version of yourself the data has seen.
